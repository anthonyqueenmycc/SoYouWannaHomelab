## Installing VirtualBox
The first step of creating a Homelab is to set up your hypervisor. In my class, we have opted to use VirtualBox due to it's wide compatibility with host and guest operating systems. <br>

1. Download VirtualBox. <br>
The first thing we need to do is go to <a href="https://www.virtualbox.org/" target="_blank">VirtualBox</a> and click on the big blue button for the downloads section.<br>
<br>
<img width="1920" height="919" alt="www.virtualbox.org" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2b09d376-ae95-4682-85cd-76a3049c7a6c" /><br>
<br>
2. On the Downloads page, click the link labeled "Windows Hosts". (If you are using a device other than the school provided laptop, choose the option that matched your Operating System and Hardware configuration.)<br>
<br>
<img width="1920" height="919" alt="Download_Virtualbox" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/8e012cb0-0ad3-4f66-a2e2-230354199119" /><br>
<br>
3. Once the installer has finished downloading, go ahead and click on it to launch it.(Where you look may vary based on your web browser. I use FireFox and the downloads show up in the upper right corner.)<br>
<br>
<img width="463" height="123" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/fe20ac4a-9811-4fff-b96e-9c98d821817b" /><br>
<br>
4. If you encouter the User Account Control (UAC) pop up, go ahead and click "Yes".<br>
